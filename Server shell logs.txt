<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Syllabus"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.hse.ru/en/ba/data/courses/301393255.html">Syllabus</a></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="or_see_this_version_of_the_syllabus">or see <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1RH-GXDP9RjLk0mS7qcyjlRmu9TZa61rM/view?usp=sharing">this version of the syllabus</a></span></h3>
<p>“Differential Equations” is a spring semester course for second-year students studying at the Faculty of Computer Sciences. It is designed to suit the requirements of the Faculty of Computer Sciences curriculum as well as UoL where DE is a part of the mathematical curriculum. Besides the course on differential equations is included as a topic in “Mathematical methods for economists” external exam. 
</p><p>This course is an important part of the bachelor stage in the education of the future applied mathematicians and computer scientists. It has to give students skills for the implementation of mathematical knowledge and expertise. Its prerequisite is the knowledge of the single variable calculus. 
</p><p>In the spring semester, the course is split into two unequal parts: it is taught from January through the end of April and after the students finish with their UoL exams in May it will resume and will continue till mid-June. 
</p><p>The assessment of the students will be done by setting mock exam by the end of the 3rd module, then later by the University of London (UoL) examinations in May and final exam will be set in late June. But the final grade will depend solely on mock, final exams performance and home assignments grades.
</p><p>As a general policy, personal computing devices such as laptops, calculators etc. are <b> not supposed to be used in the course</b>. They are <b> absolutely prohibited in all exams</b>. Students are expected to do all necessary arithmetic computations by hand.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overall_Results">Overall Results</span></h2>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grading_System">Grading System</span></h2>
<p>The home assignments constitute 30% of the final grade. The final exam is 40% of the final grade. The rest of the grade is formed by Midterm test which is due in the end of the 3rd module.
</p><p><b> The final grade on the course</b> will be determined according to the formula:
</p><p><b>Grade</b> = 0.4 * Final Exam + 0.3 * Midterm test + 0.3 * Home assignments
</p><p>All grades are given initially out of 100. The final grades are also transferred to 10- and 5-points grades in accordance with the table (to be posted).
3.      The scale of corresponding grades
3.1.    Total grades for a given course are converted from the 100-point scale to the 5-point
and 10-point scale.
3.2.    The following basic scale has been established to convert grades from the 100-point scale to the 10-point scale
100-point scale 10-point scale
</p><p>0-19,99   -&gt;    1
</p><p>20-29,99 -&gt;   2
</p><p>30-39,99 -&gt;   3
</p><p>40-46,99  -&gt;  4
</p><p>47-53,99 -&gt;   5
</p><p>54-61,99    -&gt;     6
</p><p>62-69,99 -&gt;   7
</p><p>70-77,99    -&gt;   8
</p><p>78-85,9 -&gt; 9
</p><p>86-100   -&gt;       10
</p><p><br />
Retakes are organized in accordance with the HSE Interim and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.hse.ru/docs/231102058.html">Ongoing  Assessment Regulations</a>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9E_.D0.BA.D1.83.D1.80.D1.81.D0.B5">О курсе</span></h2>
<p><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC8PQpPltnaMvlr6g1wkkTqw/featured">Youtube-канал с лекциями</a>
</p><p><b>Оценивание</b>
</p><p>UoL = Экзамен * 0.7 + Письменная (или курсовая) работа * 0.3 
</p><p>Итоговая оценка = Экзамен * 0.5 + 0.3 * Контрольная работа + 0.2 * Домашняя работа 
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Module_1">Module 1</span></h4>
<p>(1 lecture + 1 class per group X 7 weeks)
</p>
<ul>
<li> Bootstrap
</li>
<li> Welch test
</li>
<li> Mann-Whitney test
</li>
<li> CUPED
</li>
<li> Difference in Difference estimator
</li>
<li> Matching
</li>
<li> Multiple comparison corrections
</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Module_2">Module 2</span></h4>
<p>(1 lecture + 1 class per group X 8 weeks)
</p>
<ul>
<li> Sampling
</li>
<li> Sample size calculation
</li>
<li> Contingency tables, Chi-squared tests
</li>
<li> ANOVA, ANCOVA
</li>
<li> Partial correlation
</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Module_3">Module 3</span></h4>
<p>(1 lecture + 1 class per group X 11 weeks)
</p>
<ul>
<li> Discriminant analysis
</li>
<li> Logit
</li>
<li> PCA
</li>
<li> Factor analysis
</li>
<li> Cluster analysis, Dendrogramms
</li>
<li> Conjoint Analysis
</li>
<li> Multidimensional scaling
</li>
</ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.91.D0.BE.D0.B5.D0.B2.D0.BE.D0.B9_.D0.BB.D0.B8.D1.81.D1.82.D0.BE.D0.BA:_.D1.81.D0.B5.D0.BC.D0.B5.D1.81.D1.82.D1.80_II">Боевой листок: семестр II</span></h2>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9B.D0.B5.D0.BA.D1.86.D0.B8.D1.8F_1._.D0.A2.D0.B0.D0.B1.D0.BB.D0.B8.D1.86.D1.8B_.D1.81.D0.BE.D0.BF.D1.80.D1.8F.D0.B6.D0.B5.D0.BD.D0.BD.D0.BE.D1.81.D1.82.D0.B8">Лекция 1. Таблицы сопряженности</span></h4>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9B.D0.B5.D0.BA.D1.86.D0.B8.D1.8F_2._.D0.94.D0.B8.D1.81.D0.BA.D1.80.D0.B8.D0.BC.D0.B8.D0.BD.D0.B0.D0.BD.D1.82.D0.BD.D1.8B.D0.B9_.D0.B0.D0.BD.D0.B0.D0.BB.D0.B8.D0.B7">Лекция 2. Дискриминантный анализ</span></h4>
<ul>
<li> Дискриминантный анализ — генеративная модель: f(y, x). 
</li>
<li> LDA, QDA, MDA.
</li>
<li> Выводим границу для LDA.
</li>
<li> Хорошие слайды Jia Li: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://personal.psu.edu/jol2/course/stat597e/notes2/lda.pdf">lda, qda</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://personal.psu.edu/jol2/course/stat597e/notes2/mda.pdf">mda</a>
</li>
<li> LDA для снижения размерности <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://sebastianraschka.com/Articles/2014_python_lda.html">lda bit by bit</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.sjsu.edu/faculty/guangliang.chen/Math253S20/lec11lda.pdf">Lecture by Chen</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://sites.stat.washington.edu/wxs/Stat592-w2011/Slides/cancorr-notes.pdf">Link to Canonical correlation</a>
</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9B.D0.B5.D0.BA.D1.86.D0.B8.D1.8F_3._.D0.9B.D0.BE.D0.B3.D0.B8.D1.82">Лекция 3. Логит</span></h4>
<ul>
<li> Логит — дискриминирующая модель: f(y|x). В отличие от дискриминантного анализа&#160;:)
</li>
<li> F' = F(1-F)
</li>
<li> При кодировке 0/1 сумма прогнозных вероятностей равна количеству 1. 
</li>
<li> Проблема совершенной разделимости.
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9F.D0.BE.D0.BB.D0.B5.D0.B7.D0.BD.D1.8B.D0.B5_.D0.B8.D1.81.D1.82.D0.BE.D1.87.D0.BD.D0.B8.D0.BA.D0.B8_.D0.B2_.D0.BF.D0.B5.D1.80.D0.B5.D0.BC.D0.B5.D1.88.D0.BA.D1.83">Полезные источники в перемешку</span></h2>
<p>General AB:
</p>
<ul>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://vkteam.medium.com/practitioners-guide-to-statistical-tests-ed2d580ef04f">VK practitioner guide</a>
</li>
<li> Deng, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://alexdeng.github.io/public/files/wsdm2017-rup.pdf">Trustworthy analysis of online A/B tests: Pitfalls, challenges and solutions</a>
</li>
<li> Deng, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://alexdeng.github.io/">webpage</a> 
</li>
<li> Stats SE, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://stats.stackexchange.com/questions/398436/a-b-testing-ratio-of-sums">AB testing for ratio of sums</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://towardsdatascience.com/a-b-testing-a-complete-guide-to-statistical-testing-e3f1db140499">Guide to AB</a>. Тесты для таблиц сопряжённости, Welch test, Mann-Whitney.
</li>
</ul>
<p>Bootstrap:
</p>
<ul>
<li> Tim Hestenberg, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1411.5279">What teachers should know about the bootstrap</a>. Лучшее первое знакомство с бутстрэпом. Если есть время на один источник по бутстрэпу, то это то, что нужно.
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~jordan/sail/readings/edgeworth.pdf">Edgeworth expansion</a>. Большая книжка с брутальной теорией, преобразованиями Фурье, доказательствами и картинкой с матрёшками.
</li>
<li> James MacKinnon, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/6494253.pdf">Bootstrap Methods in Econometrics (про дикий и парный бутстрэп)</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.unofficialgoogledatascience.com/2015/08/an-introduction-to-poisson-bootstrap26.html">Poisson bootstrap</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1304.7406">Evaluation of Bootstrap Methods</a>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Робастные ошибки в регрессии:
</p>
<ul>
<li> James Mackinnon, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/254447243_Thirty_Years_of_Heteroskedasticity-Robust_Inference">30 years of robust inference</a>
</li>
</ul>
<p>CUPED + CUPAC:
</p>
<ul>
<li> Deng, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://exp-platform.com/Documents/2013-02-CUPED-ImprovingSensitivityOfControlledExperiments.pdf">Improving the Sensitivity of Online Experiments, CUPED</a>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Оригинальная статья, на CUPED можно смотреть как на две подряд применённых регрессии&#160;:)
</p>
<ul>
<li> Victor Lei, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://booking.ai/how-booking-com-increases-the-power-of-online-experiments-with-cuped-995d186fff1d">How booking increases power with CUPED</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/345698207_Control_Using_Predictions_as_Covariates_in_Switchback_Experiments">CUPAC</a>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Де-факто: признак генерируется любым алгоритмом машинного обучения, затем множественная регрессия с кластер-робастными стандартными ошибками.
</p><p><br />
Linearization + Delta-method:
</p>
<ul>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3159652.3159699">Transformation of Ratio Metrics</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.stat.cmu.edu/~hseltman/files/ratio.pdf">Approximate E, Var for ratio</a>
</li>
<li> Deng, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.06336">Applying delta method:</a> practical guide with novel ideas
</li>
</ul>
<p><br />
Эффекты воздействия:
</p>
<ul>
<li> Рубен Ениколопоп, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://quantile.ru/06/06-RE.pdf">Эффекты воздействия</a>. Мэтчинг, propensity score.
</li>
<li> Matheus Facure, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://matheusfacure.github.io/python-causality-handbook/landing-page.html">Causal inference for Brave and True</a>. Инструментальные переменные, мэтчинг, RDD и прочие попытки выяснить причинно-следственные связи на неэкспериментальных данных. С мемасиками и в питоне.
</li>
</ul>

<h1><span class="mw-headline" id="Abstract">Abstract</span></h1>
<p>The course gives a comprehensive foundation for theory, methods and algorithms of mathematical optimization. The prerequisites are 
linear algebra and calculus.
</p>
<h1><span class="mw-headline" id="Learning_Objectives">Learning Objectives</span></h1>
<p>- Students will study the main concepts of optimization theory and develop a methodology for theoretical investigation of optimization problems.
</p><p>- Students will obtain an understanding of creation, effectiveness and application optimization methods and algorithms on practice.
</p><p>- The course will give students the possibility of solving standard and nonstandard mathematical problems connected to finding optimal solutions.
</p>
<h1><span class="mw-headline" id="Expected_Learning_Outcomes">Expected Learning Outcomes</span></h1>
<p>- Students should be able to classify optimization problems according to their mathematical properties.
</p><p>- Students should be able to perform a theoretical investigation of a given optimization problem in order to assess its complexity.
</p><p>- Students should be able to write down first and second-order optimality conditions.
</p><p>- Students should be able to provide a dual analysis of linear and convex optimization problems.
</p><p>- Students should be able to assess the rate of convergence of the first and second-order optimization methods.
</p><p>- Students should be able to solve simple optimization problems without a computer.
</p><p>- Students should be able to implement different optimization codes in a computer environment.
</p><p>- Students should be able to analyse the obtained solutions.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h1><span class="mw-headline" id="Course_content">Course content</span></h1>
<p>- Unvariate optimization: unimodal (invex) functions, golden section method, Lipschitzian optimization.
</p><p>- Unconstrained quadratic optimization: algebraic approach, complete characterization of stationary points, gradient method with exact line search, conjugate gradient method.
</p><p>- General unconstrained optimization: first and second-order optimality conditions, descent directions, steepest descent method, conjugate gradient methods, Newton method, quasi-Newton methods, inexact line search, rate of convergence.
</p><p>- Optimization problems with linear equality constraints: first and second-order optimality conditions, Lagrange function.
</p><p>- General smooth optimization problems with equality and inequality constraints: first and second-order optimality conditions, dual problems, penalty and augmented Lagrangian methods, sequential quadratic programming.
</p><p>- Convex optimization: optimality conditions, duality, subgradients and subdifferential, cutting planes and bundle methods, the complexity of convex optimization.
</p><p>- Linear programming: duality, decomposition.
</p><p>- Interior-point methods.
</p>
<h1><span class="mw-headline" id="Recommended_Bibliography">Recommended Bibliography</span></h1>
<p>- Nocedal J., Wright S.J. Numerical optimization. Second Edition. Springer, 2006 
</p><p>- Bazaraa M. S., Sherali H.D, Shetty C. M. Nonlinear Programming: Theory and Algorithms 3rd Edition, John Wiley &amp; Sons, 2006
</p><p>- Beck, A. First-Order Methods in Optimization, MOS-SIAM Series on Optimization, 2017
</p><p>- Ben-Tal A., Arkadi Nemirovski A. Lectures on Modern Convex Optimization: Analysis, Algorithms, and Engineering Applications, MOS-SIAM Series on Optimization, 2001
</p><p>- Nesterov Yu. Introductory Lectures on Convex Optimization, Springer US, 2004
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Syllabus"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.hse.ru/en/ba/data/courses/301393255.html">Syllabus</a></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="or_see_this_version_of_the_syllabus">or see <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1RH-GXDP9RjLk0mS7qcyjlRmu9TZa61rM/view?usp=sharing">this version of the syllabus</a></span></h3>
<p>“Differential Equations” is a spring semester course for second-year students studying at the Faculty of Computer Sciences. It is designed to suit the requirements of the Faculty of Computer Sciences curriculum as well as UoL where DE is a part of the mathematical curriculum. Besides the course on differential equations is included as a topic in “Mathematical methods for economists” external exam. 
</p><p>This course is an important part of the bachelor stage in the education of the future applied mathematicians and computer scientists. It has to give students skills for the implementation of mathematical knowledge and expertise. Its prerequisite is the knowledge of the single variable calculus. 
</p><p>In the spring semester, the course is split into two unequal parts: it is taught from January through the end of April and after the students finish with their UoL exams in May it will resume and will continue till mid-June. 
</p><p>The assessment of the students will be done by setting mock exam by the end of the 3rd module, then later by the University of London (UoL) examinations in May and final exam will be set in late June. But the final grade will depend solely on mock, final exams performance and home assignments grades.
</p><p>As a general policy, personal computing devices such as laptops, calculators etc. are <b> not supposed to be used in the course</b>. They are <b> absolutely prohibited in all exams</b>. Students are expected to do all necessary arithmetic computations by hand.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overall_Results">Overall Results</span></h2>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grading_System">Grading System</span></h2>
<p>The home assignments constitute 30% of the final grade. The final exam is 40% of the final grade. The rest of the grade is formed by Midterm test which is due in the end of the 3rd module.
</p><p><b> The final grade on the course</b> will be determined according to the formula:
</p><p><b>Grade</b> = 0.4 * Final Exam + 0.3 * Midterm test + 0.3 * Home assignments
</p><p>All grades are given initially out of 100. The final grades are also transferred to 10- and 5-points grades in accordance with the table (to be posted).
3.      The scale of corresponding grades
3.1.    Total grades for a given course are converted from the 100-point scale to the 5-point
and 10-point scale.
3.2.    The following basic scale has been established to convert grades from the 100-point scale to the 10-point scale
100-point scale 10-point scale
</p><p>0-19,99   -&gt;    1
</p><p>20-29,99 -&gt;   2
</p><p>30-39,99 -&gt;   3
</p><p>40-46,99  -&gt;  4
</p><p>47-53,99 -&gt;   5
</p><p>54-61,99    -&gt;     6
</p><p>62-69,99 -&gt;   7
</p><p>70-77,99    -&gt;   8
</p><p>78-85,9 -&gt; 9
</p><p>86-100   -&gt;       10
</p><p><br />
Retakes are organized in accordance with the HSE Interim and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.hse.ru/docs/231102058.html">Ongoing  Assessment Regulations</a>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Syllabus"><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.hse.ru/en/ba/data/courses/301393255.html">Syllabus</a></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="or_see_this_version_of_the_syllabus">or see <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1RH-GXDP9RjLk0mS7qcyjlRmu9TZa61rM/view?usp=sharing">this version of the syllabus</a></span></h3>
<p>“Differential Equations” is a spring semester course for second-year students studying at the Faculty of Computer Sciences. It is designed to suit the requirements of the Faculty of Computer Sciences curriculum as well as UoL where DE is a part of the mathematical curriculum. Besides the course on differential equations is included as a topic in “Mathematical methods for economists” external exam. 
</p><p>This course is an important part of the bachelor stage in the education of the future applied mathematicians and computer scientists. It has to give students skills for the implementation of mathematical knowledge and expertise. Its prerequisite is the knowledge of the single variable calculus. 
</p><p>In the spring semester, the course is split into two unequal parts: it is taught from January through the end of April and after the students finish with their UoL exams in May it will resume and will continue till mid-June. 
</p><p>The assessment of the students will be done by setting mock exam by the end of the 3rd module, then later by the University of London (UoL) examinations in May and final exam will be set in late June. But the final grade will depend solely on mock, final exams performance and home assignments grades.
</p><p>As a general policy, personal computing devices such as laptops, calculators etc. are <b> not supposed to be used in the course</b>. They are <b> absolutely prohibited in all exams</b>. Students are expected to do all necessary arithmetic computations by hand.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Grading_System">Grading System</span></h2>
<p>The home assignments constitute 30% of the final grade. The final exam is 40% of the final grade. The rest of the grade is formed by Midterm test which is due in the end of the 3rd module.
</p><p><b> The final grade on the course</b> will be determined according to the formula:
</p><p><b>Grade</b> = 0.4 * Final Exam + 0.3 * Midterm test + 0.3 * Home assignments

3.      The scale of corresponding grades
3.1.    Total grades for a given course are converted from the 100-point scale to the 5-point
and 10-point scale.
3.2.    The following basic scale has been established to convert grades from the 100-point scale to the 10-point scale

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.91.D0.BE.D0.B5.D0.B2.D0.BE.D0.B9_.D0.BB.D0.B8.D1.81.D1.82.D0.BE.D0.BA:_.D1.81.D0.B5.D0.BC.D0.B5.D1.81.D1.82.D1.80_II">Боевой листок: семестр II</span></h2>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9B.D0.B5.D0.BA.D1.86.D0.B8.D1.8F_1._.D0.A2.D0.B0.D0.B1.D0.BB.D0.B8.D1.86.D1.8B_.D1.81.D0.BE.D0.BF.D1.80.D1.8F.D0.B6.D0.B5.D0.BD.D0.BD.D0.BE.D1.81.D1.82.D0.B8">Лекция 1. Таблицы сопряженности</span></h4>
<ul>
<li> Стандарт: LR тест. 
</li>
<li> Историческая ценность: хи-квадрат критерий Пирсона. 
</li>
<li> Тест на независимость.
</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9B.D0.B5.D0.BA.D1.86.D0.B8.D1.8F_2._.D0.94.D0.B8.D1.81.D0.BA.D1.80.D0.B8.D0.BC.D0.B8.D0.BD.D0.B0.D0.BD.D1.82.D0.BD.D1.8B.D0.B9_.D0.B0.D0.BD.D0.B0.D0.BB.D0.B8.D0.B7">Лекция 2. Дискриминантный анализ</span></h4>
<ul>
<li> Дискриминантный анализ — генеративная модель: f(y, x). 
</li>
<li> LDA, QDA, MDA.
</li>
<li> Выводим границу для LDA.
</li>
<li> Хорошие слайды Jia Li: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://personal.psu.edu/jol2/course/stat597e/notes2/lda.pdf">lda, qda</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://personal.psu.edu/jol2/course/stat597e/notes2/mda.pdf">mda</a>
</li>
<li> LDA для снижения размерности <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://sebastianraschka.com/Articles/2014_python_lda.html">lda bit by bit</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.sjsu.edu/faculty/guangliang.chen/Math253S20/lec11lda.pdf">Lecture by Chen</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://sites.stat.washington.edu/wxs/Stat592-w2011/Slides/cancorr-notes.pdf">Link to Canonical correlation</a>
</li>
</ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9B.D0.B5.D0.BA.D1.86.D0.B8.D1.8F_3._.D0.9B.D0.BE.D0.B3.D0.B8.D1.82">Лекция 3. Логит</span></h4>
<ul>
<li> Логит — дискриминирующая модель: f(y|x). В отличие от дискриминантного анализа&#160;:)
</li>
<li> F' = F(1-F)
</li>
<li> При кодировке 0/1 сумма прогнозных вероятностей равна количеству 1. 
</li>
<li> Проблема совершенной разделимости.
</li>
</ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id=".D0.9F.D0.BE.D0.BB.D0.B5.D0.B7.D0.BD.D1.8B.D0.B5_.D0.B8.D1.81.D1.82.D0.BE.D1.87.D0.BD.D0.B8.D0.BA.D0.B8_.D0.B2_.D0.BF.D0.B5.D1.80.D0.B5.D0.BC.D0.B5.D1.88.D0.BA.D1.83">Полезные источники в перемешку</span></h2>
<p>General AB:
</p>
<ul>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://vkteam.medium.com/practitioners-guide-to-statistical-tests-ed2d580ef04f">VK practitioner guide</a>
</li>
<li> Deng, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://alexdeng.github.io/public/files/wsdm2017-rup.pdf">Trustworthy analysis of online A/B tests: Pitfalls, challenges and solutions</a>
</li>
<li> Deng, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://alexdeng.github.io/">webpage</a> 
</li>
<li> Stats SE, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://stats.stackexchange.com/questions/398436/a-b-testing-ratio-of-sums">AB testing for ratio of sums</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://towardsdatascience.com/a-b-testing-a-complete-guide-to-statistical-testing-e3f1db140499">Guide to AB</a>. Тесты для таблиц сопряжённости, Welch test, Mann-Whitney.
</li>
</ul>
<p>Bootstrap:
</p>
<ul>
<li> Tim Hestenberg, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1411.5279">What teachers should know about the bootstrap</a>. Лучшее первое знакомство с бутстрэпом. Если есть время на один источник по бутстрэпу, то это то, что нужно.
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~jordan/sail/readings/edgeworth.pdf">Edgeworth expansion</a>. Большая книжка с брутальной теорией, преобразованиями Фурье, доказательствами и картинкой с матрёшками.
</li>
<li> James MacKinnon, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://core.ac.uk/download/pdf/6494253.pdf">Bootstrap Methods in Econometrics (про дикий и парный бутстрэп)</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.unofficialgoogledatascience.com/2015/08/an-introduction-to-poisson-bootstrap26.html">Poisson bootstrap</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1304.7406">Evaluation of Bootstrap Methods</a>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Робастные ошибки в регрессии:
</p>
<ul>
<li> James Mackinnon, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/254447243_Thirty_Years_of_Heteroskedasticity-Robust_Inference">30 years of robust inference</a>
</li>
</ul>
<p>CUPED + CUPAC:
</p>
<ul>
<li> Deng, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://exp-platform.com/Documents/2013-02-CUPED-ImprovingSensitivityOfControlledExperiments.pdf">Improving the Sensitivity of Online Experiments, CUPED</a>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Оригинальная статья, на CUPED можно смотреть как на две подряд применённых регрессии&#160;:)
</p>
<ul>
<li> Victor Lei, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://booking.ai/how-booking-com-increases-the-power-of-online-experiments-with-cuped-995d186fff1d">How booking increases power with CUPED</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.researchgate.net/publication/345698207_Control_Using_Predictions_as_Covariates_in_Switchback_Experiments">CUPAC</a>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Де-факто: признак генерируется любым алгоритмом машинного обучения, затем множественная регрессия с кластер-робастными стандартными ошибками.
</p><p><br />
Linearization + Delta-method:
</p>
<ul>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3159652.3159699">Transformation of Ratio Metrics</a>
</li>
<li> <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.stat.cmu.edu/~hseltman/files/ratio.pdf">Approximate E, Var for ratio</a>
</li>
<li> Deng, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.06336">Applying delta method:</a> practical guide with novel ideas
</li>
</ul>
<p><br />
Эффекты воздействия:
</p>
<ul>
<li> Рубен Ениколопоп, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://quantile.ru/06/06-RE.pdf">Эффекты воздействия</a>. Мэтчинг, propensity score.
</li>
<li> Matheus Facure, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://matheusfacure.github.io/python-causality-handbook/landing-page.html">Causal inference for Brave and True</a>. Инструментальные переменные, мэтчинг, RDD и прочие попытки выяснить причинно-следственные связи на неэкспериментальных данных. С мемасиками и в питоне.
</li>
</ul>
